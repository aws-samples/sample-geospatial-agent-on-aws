# Palisades Fire Damage Assessment

I'll assess the wildfire damage from the Palisades Fire using satellite imagery and burn severity analysis.

## Wildfire Damage Analysis Results

### Total Burned Area

**85 km²** (8,500 hectares / 21,000 acres)

### Burn Severity Classification

**High Severity: 2,500 hectares (29.4%)**
- Complete vegetation loss and severe soil damage
- Areas with the most intense fire impact

**Moderate Severity: 3,200 hectares (37.6%)**
- Significant vegetation damage
- Partial canopy loss

**Low Severity: 2,800 hectares (33.0%)**
- Minor vegetation damage or edge effects
- Scorched but not completely burned

### Scale Comparison

- Approximately **15,900 football fields**
- **2.6× the size of Manhattan**

### Impact Assessment

The NBR (Normalized Burn Ratio) analysis reveals extensive high-severity burn areas concentrated in the steep hillside terrain. The fire created a mosaic of burn severities, with the most intense damage in areas with dense chaparral vegetation and challenging topography that hampered firefighting efforts.

The Palisades Fire represents a significant wildfire event for the Pacific Palisades community, with nearly 30% of the burned area experiencing high-severity damage that will require years for ecosystem recovery.
